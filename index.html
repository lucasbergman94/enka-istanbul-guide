Key dates

  • Recruiting and permits run from winter through summer; the work-permit wait is long, and that is normal.

Three things to know first

  • You only need to apostille your documents. The school handles the Turkish translation and notarization.
  • Do not book flights until your work visa/permit is approved.
  • Most steps wait on the Turkish Ministry of Education. Slow does not mean wrong.
🧭 The relocation roadmap›

Phase 1 — Documents (start now)

Apostille these, each one individually:

  • University diploma
  • University transcripts (a registrar's letter stating the transcript is official helps)
  • Teaching certification / license
  • Proof of 2 years' experience in your subject: a letter from your previous school, on letterhead, signed. It does not need to come from your country's Ministry of Education.
  • Police / background certificate (where required)

Phase 2 — Ministry of Education pre-approval (Ön İzin)

  • A teacher-specific first step: the Ministry must pre-approve your assignment before a work permit can proceed.
  • HR submits your translated, notarized documents. Without this approval, the work visa cannot move forward.
  • It is the slowest part and depends on government timelines.

Phase 3 — Work visa application

  • Apply online and book an appointment at the Turkish mission in the country where you reside (have residency/work rights) or hold citizenship.
  • Have HR review your form before you submit.
  • Do not book the appointment until HR tells you to.

Phase 4 — Flights & arrival

  • Wait for visa/permit approval before buying flights.
  • Book your own flight (economy), into Istanbul Airport (IST).
  • You generally cannot arrive before the contract start without a tourist-visa workaround. Arrival support may be unavailable in July.
  • Bringing a pet? Start the rabies titer test and paperwork early; most pets fly in-cabin.

Phase 5 — First weeks in Istanbul

  • Get a Turkish SIM in your own name. This starts the 120-day clock to register your foreign phone's IMEI.
  • Get your tax number (vergi numarası): free, online.
  • Buy and load an İstanbulkart.
  • Set up your e-Devlet password at a PTT (post office).
  • Open a bank account.
  • Register your address (adres kayıt) within the required window.
  • Register your phone's IMEI within 120 days, or buy a local phone.
📄 Documents & apostille›
  • What to apostille: diploma, transcripts, teaching certificate, proof-of-experience letter (plus a police certificate if asked). Each gets its own apostille.
  • Proof-of-experience letter: from your previous school, on letterhead, signed.
  • US: apostille via the issuing state's Secretary of State, or the US Dept. of State. Usually low cost per document.
  • Non-Hague country (for example Thailand): get Ministry of Foreign Affairs approval, then take it to the nearest Turkish mission.
  • Email color scans to HR; no physical originals; no Turkish translation needed (the school does it).
  • Use a regular hard-copy apostille; digital e-apostilles have been refused.

🏠 Housing & commute›

Open the interactive map → The school bus runs from 9 stops to school, so pick a neighborhood you can reach a stop from easily.

  • School area: Sarıyer / İstinye. Walking-distance options include İstinye, Maslak, Emirgan.
  • Search sites: sahibinden.com/en and hepsiemlak.com/en (the map links to per-neighborhood searches).
  • Sahibinden needs a Turkish phone number to message agents: use the "call" button to get the number, then reach them on WhatsApp.
  • Switch the Sahibinden app to English: "bana özel" (bottom) → gear (uygulama ayarları) → dil tercihleri.
  • Furnished is scarce; the norm is unfurnished. There is an IKEA run with the school bus after arrival.
  • Do not pay a year upfront for a place you have not seen. If a landlord only rents on those terms, pass.
  • Common approach: arrive, stay in an Airbnb, and view places in person before signing.
  • Departing teachers list their apartments (HR shares a document).
🧰 Arrival setup›
  • Tax number (vergi numarası): free, online at the Interactive Tax Office.
  • e-Devlet: government services portal; get your password at a PTT.
  • Bank account: passport, tax number, proof of address, contract.
  • SIM / phone: Turkish SIM in your name; register your IMEI within 120 days or buy a local phone.
  • İstanbulkart: buy at the airport or metro; personalize it with your ID number.
  • Address registration (adres kayıt): within the required window after arrival.
  • Health letter: a doctor's note stating you are fit to work (a generic form is fine).
🌆 Daily life in Istanbul›
  • Transport: metro, metrobüs and ferries with İstanbulkart, plus the school service bus.
  • Apps: Moovit/Citymapper, BiTaksi (taxi), Yemeksepeti/Getir (delivery), Google Translate (offline Turkish).
  • Pets: rabies titer test to enter; most fly in-cabin.
  • Sport: teacher discount at the ENKA sports center.
  • Emergency: 112. Locate your nearest hospital and a nöbetçi eczane (on-duty pharmacy).

❓ FAQ›
  • When will my visa be approved? It takes time (often weeks) and depends on the Ministry. The school tracks it.
  • Where do I apply? In the country where you have residency/work rights, or your country of citizenship.
  • Do I need to translate my documents? No. The school translates them in Turkey. You only apostille.
  • Physical documents or scans? Color scans by email; do not mail your diploma.
  • Is an e-apostille okay? Use a regular apostille; digital ones have been refused.
  • When can I book my flight? After your visa/permit is approved. Book your own, into IST.
  • Can I arrive early? Generally no, without a tourist-visa workaround.
  • Sahibinden without a Turkish number? Use "call" to get the number, then WhatsApp.
  • Furnished apartments? Rare; plan to furnish (an IKEA run is available).
  • Bringing a pet? Start the rabies titer test early; finalize after the flight is booked.
